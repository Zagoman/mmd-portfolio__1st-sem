Basic Content

In Basic content we had a lot of opportunities to experiment with video content creation, it being a chance to make a story without words, with a time cap of 60 seconds, or making videos to a client trying to accommodate the client’s needs.

A story without words

In this module, we were introduced to basic techniques of video making and video editing. Being presented with very few notes on video making and storytelling, gave me a certain doubt in whether I could come up with something creative, but I believe the constraints worked as a way to drive me into a creative mindset, and a storyteller. I was able to create a video with a deep meaning, and I was able to share my message in under 60 seconds, which made it so much more useful since there would be more people willing to watch the content.

I didn’t get to experience many close-ups, since they weren’t needed to share my point across the video, but I was able to use some middle shots, to set the environment along with showing my emotions, actions, and reactions. The addition of sound wasn’t complex, since I didn’t need much more than ambient sound, and a police car siren sound.

Client brief

Preparing for the client meeting was an interesting experience, since most of the things I was worried about beforehand (like technical questions), the clients weren’t so interested in. I had spent a big amount of time thinking about whether the video should be displayed on Facebook rather than on Instagram, and thinking about details of how to handle the videos/website to them. What I learned from this experience is that I didn’t take much of the problem into consideration, and went straight into trying to figure out what solution they would like best.

I’ve had many client meetings before having the one we had in class, and none of them was like this one, because most of the time the meetings were just me and my clients, I didn’t have other 50 classmates with me to ask questions. Needless to say, I wouldn’t have much control over the situation, or which direction I wanted to direct the meeting towards, since I had no clue what the next question would be, or when I would have the opportunity to ask another question.

During the client interview, I learned a lot about their priorities, goals, and struggles. They showed that they wouldn’t have much time to spend on the project themselves, therefore we tried to minimize the amount of input we would need from them after the meeting, so in consequence, we spent a lot of time planning, brainstorming and diverging, before we could settle into finally converging and driving our project into a concrete solution.

Personas

Personas were one of the most important things I learned in this theme. I was able to understand the importance of being able to relate to someone when it comes to making decisions that could benefit them. Personas are a data-driven representation of a non-existent person that could belong to one of the target groups of the product.

In this project we went with two personas, Maya and Bjørn, they were very different from each other, and also represented two different target groups. Maya was a young Italian lady, studying finance at CBS, she was busy with school, but would be able to take the opportunity to meet people at the fridge, or donate to local people. Bjørn was different, he was an older man, in his 60s, he was widowed and wanted a way to interact with other people, so he would donate and volunteer at the fridge whenever he could with his Christiania bike.

Pre-production

After coming up with a few video ideas, we had to dial down to have just a few, to make sure we would have time to execute them all. To select the two videos we were going to shoot, we made an impact analysis, to be able to understand how our personas would be affected by each video, and if the video made sense to the target audience of the website.

After selecting the videos, we were introduced to the Story Board, where we had to have very detailed information about each shot we would shoot on the shooting day. So as a group effort, we met down at Absalon and came up with the Story Board together. I decided on using a tool that I found on the internet to create the storyboard, and although it looked good, it wasn’t representing our idea of each shot properly (According to feedback).

I also learned the importance of preparing beforehand, since we had made a Story Board, but we hadn’t anticipated all the obstacles we would have. An example of not preparing too well, was in the shots we had to do at the fridge, we didn’t that there would be so many people there, and they would be in the shot.

Customer Pitch

We had to present our Customer Pitch twice, due to the clients not being able to be present on the scheduled day. Although inconvenient, it was really nice to have the opportunity to present it to a teacher beforehand, and get feedback and have the opportunity to do a better job.

We used the PetchaKutcha presentation method, which consists of 20 slides of 20 seconds each, with that constraint we were able to make our presentation focused only on important points, and ditching small and irrelevant details, like what each of the group members did, and what method we used where. We took the decisions on what to cut and what to keep based on the fact that we would be presenting to the clients, and not to the teachers.

A website with a video

Designing the website was a joined task, we wanted to be able to get as many people on big decisions as we could, but keep not so many people executing on them at a single time, since we had so many other tasks to complete. We chose to go with a looping video on the home page, choosing to keep the message on the pathos side, appealing to the emotional side of viewers.

Our website had bright and vibrant colours, which were a request from the client’s brief, but we changed their original colours since they made their brand look outdated and not so pleasing. Our logo gives the idea of sharing, but not only food since the clients think about expanding their impact shortly.

Our second video shows someone walking alone, with a bag of food, this person is initially sad, but then turns happy once she is presented with the opportunity of donating and joining a volunteering community. Our whole idea for this video was to show the sense of community, showing the viewers how good it actually is to donate, and also how good it is to volunteer and participate in a community of people who are trying to make their community a better place.
