Basic UX

In this Theme I learned a lot about not fully trusting my instincts, instead, I should research and find information and problems that will drive my solutions. Learning about research gave me a lot of useful tools that I started using even in my professional life, and learning about when I should be converging and when I should be diverging, gave me enough understanding of project management to carry on successfully into small projects in my life.

Data Collection

In this module we were introduced to many research types, them being Desk Research, Observation Research, Interview, and Survey. We used all these methods to grab as much information as we could so we could come up with a solution to a UX Problem inside of a T-shirt selling business. In this module, I chose to dive into t-shirt production aspects, and find flaws and aspects that needed Improvement.

Learnings

Research

I used Desk Research, which is a method of finding information that has already been researched by others, to find information about t-shirt production and to find UX problems into which I would like to dive deeper into in future assignments. I found information about the industry’s wasteful ways to come up with the clothing you see at the stores, regardless of whether the store lives in an online environment, or in a physical space.

Findings

For the assignment we had, I didn’t find much use for the Observation Research, since most of the data I acquired was quantitative, and not having done the research for a long period might not have given me any taste or preferences direct related to a specific demographic. Perhaps the most useful data I found was that 60% of the people wearing t-shirts that day were male, but we didn’t find the data trustworthy enough to base our future product on this fact.

Early Prototypes

In my opinion, this part of the Theme was one the most useful ones and was crucial in keeping the team working towards the same goal. We had multiple activities, that later on the theme was separated into Convergent and Divergent categories. Some of these activities were Lightning Demos, Crazy 8s, Sketching, Brainstorming...

Learnings

Project Management

Having a go with a team for the first time in the semester was a bit scary, but quickly everything fell into place with the fast brainstorming activities we had to do. Having specific ways to brainstorm gave us more direction, and gave us the control that we needed to come up with a solution that made sense. I was chosen to be in charge of Project Management, even though we hadn’t gone too in-depth into this aspect. One of the most important things I learned performing this role, was to be able to step back in minor decisions (like minimal design choices, and colour choosing), and to be able to look at the bigger picture of where we are within our goals, and what else needs to be done or planned to reach it.

Storyboarding

A storyboard is a tool that helps us to understand who is going to be using our project, and the actions this user is going to be taking. We created our storyboard with the findings that we had of most of the people using their phones to reach clothing websites. Our storyboard starts with our user finding an Ad of our product being worn by one of their favourite artists, then the user clicks on the ad, entering our website and performing all the purchasing flow.

Think Aloud Test

We also had the privilege to do a Think Aloud Test, which we found very useful. The think-aloud test is made for us to be able to see the user interacting with our solution, and in this phase, the product we had tested was our paper prototype, which gave us a lot of insights on buttons sizes, purchase flow, and usage.

Solution

Coming up with our solution based on facts and data wasn’t an easy task, but it taught me a lot about not jumping to conclusions, and also not assuming that we have any answers.

We created a webshop in a mobile version since our target audience would be finding us through social media artists on Instagram, and other media. We would only display 6 t-shirts from each of the 2 current artists, and all the t-shirts would be pre-order. These choices were made with the conscious decision of decreasing the textile waste, by only producing what was going to go to the consumer’s closet. Giving the users limitations was our solution to stop doom scrolling in these platforms, and also to decrease the indecisiveness factor.

Pitch Deck

Creating a pitch deck was interesting, we worked hard to create a story, so it wouldn’t be a boring slideshow presentation. We used tools that were presented to us, like the dramaturgical graph that shows where you should place the Climax, the hook, the PONR, and where we should have the mounting tension. These graphics along with the hero’s journey helped us to have an interesting presentation while giving enough information to keep the audience hooked.
