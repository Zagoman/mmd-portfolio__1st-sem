A little about me and my upbringing

I was born in Brazil, and have moved around many times, having High School, and Summer Camp experiences in the USA, living and working in New Zealand, becoming a citizen of Italy, and more. I carry my roots wherever I am, and I try my best not to lose my sense of self in other cultures, but still, take as much as I can from them. So as you can guess by now, I can be a warm and welcoming person, that is what is usually assumed with my culture, but don’t get me wrong, I will know if I am being used or mistreated.

I was raised in a family of five people, three males and two females, and I am the youngest. Being the youngest gave me the opportunity to learn with my siblings mistakes, so I wouldn’t have to repeat them, and I would be able to spend time exploring other roads. I was always enrolled in many activities, I have played Basketball, I have been a circus artist, photographer, teacher and more.

What my past consists of

My life was very active in terms of physical education from 2006 to 2014, I was practicing to become a circus artist during the first half, then I changed my passion into basketball, which took me to study in an American High School. After having the High School experience, I came back to Brazil, and quickly started a Startup with my brother and a friend, we pushed our startup for almost 1 year, until we couldn’t financially support it anymore, and neither get enough funding to continue.

After having all these experiences, I decided to start travelling, and the cheapest option I could find was to travel to the USA and work as a Camp Counselor. Every summer from 2016 to 2019, I worked in summer camps, taking many different roles, from Wilderness Adventure Trip Guide to Sports Director in a Camp for people with special needs. This experience taught me a lot on how to be a leader, and also on how to work as a team. After the summer of 2019, I decided to go to New Zealand, and in there, I started another passion, climbing.

Why did I choose to study MMD

Studying Multimedia Design was never a dream of mine, but being locked down in New Zealand made me realise I needed to pick up on a skill, and for some reason, I remembered I really enjoyed my time trying to create our Startup website, so I decided to learn everything again, but this time trying to understand every little aspect of it. I quickly fell in love with Web Design and Web Development, along with falling in love with my amazing girlfriend.

My girlfriend, who I had just recently started dating at the time, is from Denmark, so I wanted to be close to her, and find a way to continue studying what I like, so she showed me KEA, and the Multimedia Design course. After failing the first attempt to get selected during the winter, I decided to apply once more, this time starting in the summer, and happily, I got accepted.
